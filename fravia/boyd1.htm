THE IMPORTANCE OF ACCMAIL
by fravia+, July 1999
Let's get straight to the point: Accessing the web per email is NOT just an 'ancient' mode to surf, it is -or rather could be - a very powerful WEAPON in our fight against the silly commercialization of the web, against powerful market-forces (an euphemism for "greedy profit-oriented sad bastards", as usual ready to transform your web-life in an advertisement clicking inferno to scrap a couple of dollars). These forces are continuously "pushing frills and punishing content" on the whole net, and dare call this 'evolution'. The final aim is a net of slaves, assembled and herded from portal to portal inside a dark tunnel of advertising banners, stroboscopic publicity displays and pushed false information. Consumer-livestock, that's what web-surfers should transform themselves into according to the pundits whose bank accounts depend on that.
Unfortunately this rant of mine does not mean much in the real (sad) society where we live. Ah! But do not despair: there are other very sound reasons to access the web per email! And I'm going to list them here for the unawares and the younger ones.

Reducing costs, saving time
The world is NOT the States. Almost everywhere local calls must be paid. Downloading (is just an example:-) that huge "softice version 4" package with its 15.000.000 bytes (Numega is going Micro$oftish :-( can take anything between 15 and 45 minutes in the European Union, who knows how long in Gabon or in Mongolia. That's time you have to pay for÷ unless you learn to use the accmail techniques.
Once more (I repeat because I know that few seem to grasp this): YES! You can download for free whatever you want, wherever you want and get it sent to you per email at the address you prefer. You can anonymously use the ftpservers or the www4 ones and get your targets on some pseudo address of yours. Let's imagine you have an Internet provider that gives to you six hour on the net every month for free, all other hours you have to pay for.
Well, six hours per month are MORE than enough if you master the accmail techniques! You can download infinite web pages, receive several mailing list and peruse them off-line, and you will also have time to learn a plethora of other interesting things that have been forgotten or are ignored by all those " only online" zombies (remailers, echoes, veronikas, gopher, fax, and so on). Your telephone bill will not grow either. And you will never loose time waiting for a slow page to download: all pages or programs or images will come by mail at the full speed of your ISP-modem connection, not at some wacky net connection with Godzilla knows where at low speed and ftp-busy risk.
Yes, no wonder that very big market forces have all interest to let you never learn of the mere existence of accmail methods. Accessing the web per email is a revolutionary technique! It relies on the "web of old" spirit, when everything was for free and for anybody to have for the sake of knowledge, not in order to be spammed by some pathetical widget-selling clown.

Revenge of the third world
Yes, the web is NOT the States.
Internet / e-mail connectivity and access is becoming cheaper (and, in some cases, free) in the developed countries (this is of course paid through the fact that it takes longer to find the information you are looking for in a sea of garbage masquerade trough frills) the case is different in the developing countries. Recent data that shows that, compared to the USA, full internet access costs 4 times more in India, 5 times more in Kenya, and 6 times more in Armenia. Based on hearsay, it can be said that the situation in other South Asian, African and Eastern Europe countries is not that different. The economical reasons listed above are even more stringent for third world inhabitants: if you are reading this now and come from a third world country, the best thing you can probably do in your whole life is to cut and pest the following snippet, print it and distribute it to all those that you meet that have only email access to the web.

Anonymity and corporate captivity
As anyone that has read my corporate page and my anonymity pages knows, there is not much you can do when you sit inside tour cubicle and all your web-acess to the world is filtered to see if you are being productive, or if you are seeking another job (as you should by all means do if you are in such an environment) or if you are looking for images on alt.lick.my.shoes.
Well, there's not much you can do, but one of the most interesting things you could do is to use some accmail techniques. Email is still considered somehow "personal".
They snoop email allright of course, but they will not be so ready to use against you your email usage, or to be catch doing it, since you could easily accuse them to snoop on your private sphere if they did. Let's put it this way: stupid and Internet-ignorant judges will (probably) condemn you for having used the Internet access gently provided by your masters in order to see evil pages, but will (probably) condemn your principal for having snooped on your holy private email.
You see now another case for accessing the web per email.
Keeping on the same anonymity track, accessing per email will also teach you quite a lot more about where you are really going and where from are coming the things you get than through web-graphical access. I won't repeat here all the concealed things that your browser does on your hard disk every time you access some site and all the things people can learn about your life and preferred search strings every time you browse around smearing your personal data allover the net. All this will be cut down accessing per email, of course. A complete anonymity is an impossible dream, but some sort of anonymity will be available at no cost.

Et de hoc satis.
